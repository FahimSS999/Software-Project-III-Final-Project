From the Garage to the Googleplex

Google began its journey in 1995 at Stanford University, founded by Larry Page and Sergey Brin. Starting as a small search engine, Google grew to become one of the world's most influential tech companies.

With groundbreaking innovations like Gmail, Google Maps, and Android, Google continues to shape the future of technology and human interaction with digital platforms.

Google's Milestones

Foundation (1995-1998)
Year Event
1995 Google's foundation began with the research project "Backrub" at Stanford University by Larry Page and Sergey Brin.
1997 Domain "google.com" was registered, marking the start of Google's journey.
1998 Google was officially founded on September 4, 1998, with initial funding from Andy Bechtolsheim.
Growth and Expansion (1999-2004)
Year Event
1999 Google secured $25 million in funding from venture capitalists, expanding operations and hiring talent.
2000 Launched AdWords, its advertising platform that became a major revenue source for Google.
2004 Google went public with an IPO, raising $1.67 billion and becoming a publicly traded company.
Major Innovations and Acquisitions (2005-2010)
Year Event
2005 Google acquired Android Inc., which became the foundation of its Android mobile operating system.
2006 Acquired YouTube for $1.65 billion, expanding into video content.
2008 Launched Google Chrome browser, marking its entry into the web browser market.
Reorganization and Diversification (2011-2014)
Year Event
2011 Launched Google+ social network in an attempt to enter the social media space.
2012 Acquired Motorola Mobility for $12.5 billion, expanding into hardware.
2014 Google restructured and became part of Alphabet Inc., with various initiatives falling under the new parent company.
Recent Developments (2015-Present)
Year Event
2015 Alphabet Inc. was officially created, with Google becoming its largest subsidiary.
2017 Google launched Google Home, a smart speaker with voice-controlled capabilities.
2020 Launched Google Stadia, a cloud gaming service, entering the gaming industry.
